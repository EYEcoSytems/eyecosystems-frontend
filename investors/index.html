Federal Deadline

The April 24, 2026 ADA Title II deadline is forcing 90,000+ state and local government entities — and every GovTech vendor serving them — to demonstrate digital accessibility compliance. The tools exist. The auditing firms exist. What doesn't exist is the structured, machine-readable community data layer that proves good faith engagement with the communities these mandates protect. That's Eyecosystems.

The Problem

A $50 Billion Market With a Data Gap

GovTech is a $50 billion market. Thousands of compliance vendors offer auditing tools, VPAT generators, and accessibility overlays. But every one of them has the same blind spot: they can tell a government entity what's wrong with their website, but they can't tell them anything about the blind and low-vision community those websites are supposed to serve.

ADA Title II compliance isn't just a technical checkbox. It requires documented good faith engagement with affected communities. That requires data — structured, verified, up-to-date data about BVI organizations, services, and resources in the jurisdiction. That data doesn't exist anywhere today. No database. No API. No vendor. Until Eyecosystems.

The Gap
Compliance tools tell governments what's broken. But they can't tell them who they're failing or how to reach them. The BVI community data layer is missing from every compliance stack in the market.
The Mandate
The DOJ's April 2026 rule requires covered entities to demonstrate good faith accessibility efforts. Good faith requires evidence. Evidence requires data. Eyecosystems is that data.
The Timing
With the deadline arriving and compliance procurement accelerating, the window to become the standard BVI community data layer for GovTech is open right now.

The Solution

Eyecosystems: The Community Data Infrastructure Layer

Eyecosystems is building a structured, human-verified knowledge graph of blind and low-vision community organizations, services, events, and resources — delivered as a REST API and bulk data product to GovTech vendors and government entities.

Every record is verified by trained BVI community members through our CLEAR verification method. The result is a data product that doesn't just describe the community — it's built by the community, auditable by regulators, and trusted by legal teams navigating DOJ scrutiny.

We launched the Jacksonville Hub as our first deployment, in partnership with the Eye Spy Foundation. We have 350+ live verified records, 10,000+ in the verification pipeline, and a clear roadmap to statewide and national expansion.

Knowledge Graph
Structured, machine-readable data about BVI organizations, events, services, and resources — with full schema documentation and compliance metadata.
Human Verification
BVI community members verify every record weekly via the CLEAR method. Verifier ID, timestamp, and method are logged per record — creating a legally defensible audit trail.
National Expansion
Jacksonville Hub is the proof of concept. The model is replicable to any metro with a BVI community partner. We are building the playbook for national scale.
